为什么我支持社会主义？——阿尔伯特·爱因斯坦

Posted on 2024-02-10 In 2024 , 2024-02 Word count in article: 7k Reading time ≈ 23 mins.

1949年5月，一本宣传社会主义的杂志，Monthly Review，在美国纽约出版。阿尔伯特·爱因斯坦为它撰写了开篇文章—— Why Socialism?

在此文中，爱因斯坦提出以下观点

  • 资本主义存在如下缺点

    • 政党在很大程度上由资本家资助或以其他方式影响。
    • 资本家不可避免地直接或间接控制着主要的信息来源（新闻、广播、教育）。
    • 当今经济虽有劳动合同限制，但与“纯粹”的资本主义没有太大区别。

  • 然而，资本主义社会的经济无序化是问题的真正根源

  • 只有一种方法可以消除这个弊端，那就是计划经济，以及配套的教育系统。

    • 计划经济，根据需求调整生产，将要做的工作分配给所有能工作的人，并保证每个男人、女人和孩子的生计。
    • 个体的教育，除了促进他自己的天赋能力外，还会试图在他身上培养对同胞的责任感，而不是在我们当前社会中对权力和成功的颂扬。

  • 计划经济本身可能伴随着个人的完全奴役（因为政治和经济权力的广泛集中）。实现社会主义需要解决一些极其困难的问题：

    • 如何保护个人的权利（自由、民主、平等）？
    • 如何对官僚权力进行制衡？

Chinese Version

对于一个不是经济学、社会学专家的人来说，表达对社会主义的看法是否明智？我认为是的，原因如下：

让我们首先从科学知识的角度考虑这个问题。看起来，天文学和经济学之间似乎没有本质的方法论差异：两个领域的科学家都试图为一组限定的现象发现普遍接受的法则，以尽可能清晰地理解这些现象之间的相互联系。但实际上，这样的方法论差异是存在的。在经济领域发现普遍法则之所以困难，是因为观察到的经济现象常常受到许多难以单独评估的因素的影响。此外，自所谓的人类文明时期开始以来积累的经验——众所周知——在很大程度上受到了并非完全是经济性质的原因的影响和限制。例如，历史上的大多数主要国家的存在归功于征服。征服者在被征服国家以法律和经济形式确立自己为特权阶级。他们为自己夺取了土地所有权的垄断，并从自己的行列中指派祭司。祭司控制教育，将社会的阶级划分变成一个永久性机构，并创造了一套价值体系，人民从此在很大程度上无意识地被引导在他们的社会行为中。

但是，历史传统可以说是昨日之事；我们实际上还没有真正克服索斯坦·韦伯伦所称的“人类发展的掠夺阶段”。我们观察到的经济事实属于那个阶段，甚至我们能从中推导出的法则也不适用于其他阶段。社会主义的真正目的正是为了克服并超越人类发展的掠夺阶段，经济科学在其当前状态下对未来的社会主义社会几乎无法提供什么启示。

其次，社会主义是朝着一个社会伦理目标前进。然而，科学不能创造目标，更不能在人类中植入目标；科学最多只能提供达成某些目标的手段。但目标本身是由具有崇高伦理理想的个性所构想的——如果这些目标不是死生，而是充满活力和活力的——就会被许多人采纳和推进，这些人在半无意识中决定了社会的缓慢演变。

因此，当涉及到人类问题时，我们应该警惕不要过分高估科学和科学方法；我们不应该假设只有专家才有权就影响社会组织的问题表达意见。

无数的声音已经在声称，人类社会正在经历一场危机，其稳定性已被严重破坏。这种情况的特点是，个体对他们所属的小组或大组感到冷漠甚至敌意。为了说明我的意思，让我在这里记录一个个人经历。我最近与一个聪明且有好意的人讨论了另一场战争的威胁，我认为这将严重危及人类的存在，并我指出只有一个超国家组织才能提供来自那种危险的保护。于是我的访客非常冷静和平静地对我说：“你为什么如此强烈反对人类的消失呢？”

我相信就在一个世纪前，没有人会如此轻率地发表这种观点。这是一个努力徒劳寻找内在平衡但或多或少失去希望的人的声明。这是许多人在当今时代所遭受的痛苦孤独和隔离感的表达。原因是什么？有出路吗？

提出这样的问题很容易，但要以任何程度的把握回答它们却很困难。然而，我必须尽我所能尝试，尽管我非常清楚我们的感受和追求往往是矛盾和模糊的，它们不能用简单的公式来表达。

人同时是一个孤独的存在和一个社会的存在。作为一个孤独的存在，他试图保护自己的生存和那些最亲近他的人的生存，满足他的个人欲望，并发展他的天赋能力。作为一个社会的存在，他寻求获得同伴的认可和爱护，分享他们的快乐，安慰他们的悲伤，并改善他们的生活条件。只有这些多种多样、经常冲突的追求，才能解释人的特殊性格，而这些追求的具体组合决定了一个人能在多大程度上实现内在平衡并为社会的福祉做出贡献。这两种驱动力的相对强度在很大程度上可能是由遗传确定的。但最终形成的个性在很大程度上是由一个人在其发展过程中所处的环境、他成长的社会的结构、该社会的传统以及对特定行为类型的评价所塑造的。抽象概念“社会”对个体人类意味着他与同时代人以及所有先前几代人的直接和间接关系的总和。个体能够自己思考、感受、奋斗和工作；但他在物理、智力和情感存在上如此依赖于社会，以至于在没有社会框架的情况下想象或理解他是不可能的。正是“社会”为人类提供食物、衣服、家园、工作工具、语言、思维形式和大部分思维内容；他的生活通过过去和现在数以百万计的人的劳动和成就变得可能，这些人都隐藏在小小的词“社会”背后。

因此，个体对社会的依赖是一种自然事实，无法消除——就像在蚂蚁和蜜蜂的情况一样。然而，虽然蚂蚁和蜜蜂的整个生命过程都被严格的、遗传的本能固定下来到最小的细节，但人类的社会模式和相互关系非常多变且容易改变。记忆、制造新组合的能力、口头沟通的礼物使人类能够实现不受生物必需品支配的发展。这样的发展在传统、机构和组织；在文学；在科学和工程成就；在艺术作品中表现出来。这解释了为什么，在某种意义上，人可以通过自己的行为影响他的生活，并且在这个过程中，有意识的思考和意愿可以发挥作用。

人在出生时通过遗传获得一种我们必须认为是固定和不变的生物构成，包括人类物种特有的自然冲动。此外，在他的一生中，他通过与社会的交流以及通过许多其他类型的影响从社会中获得一种文化构成。正是这种文化构成，随着时间的推移，会发生变化，并在很大程度上决定了个体与社会的关系。现代人类学通过对所谓的原始文化的比较研究，教会了我们人类的社会行为可能根据占主导地位的文化模式和组织类型大不相同。正是在此基础上，那些努力改善人类境况的人可以寄予希望：人类并非因其生物构成而注定要相互毁灭或处于残酷的、自我造成的命运之下。

如果我们问自己如何改变社会结构和人的文化态度，以使人类生活尽可能满意，我们应该始终意识到有些条件我们无法修改。如前所述，人的生物本性实际上不可能改变。此外，过去几个世纪的技术和人口发展创造了一些固定的条件。在相对密集的人口中，对于他们继续存在所不可缺少的商品，极端的劳动分工和高度集中的生产装置是绝对必要的。那个看似如此田园诗般的时代已经永远过去了，当时个体或相对较小的群体可以完全自给自足。说人类现在甚至构成一个行星生产和消费的社区只是略有夸张。

我现在已经到了可以简要指出我认为我们时代危机的本质所在的地方。它涉及个体与社会的关系。个体比以往任何时候都更加意识到他对社会的依赖。但他不是将这种依赖作为一种积极的资产、一种有机的联系、一种保护力量来体验，而是作为对他的自然权利甚至对他的经济存在的威胁。此外，他在社会中的地位是这样的，以至于他的构成中的自私驱动被不断强调，而他的社会驱动力，本质上较弱，逐渐恶化。社会中的所有人，无论他们在社会中的地位如何，都在遭受这一恶化过程的苦难。他们不知不觉地成为自己自私主义的囚犯，感到不安全、孤独和被剥夺了生活的天真、简单和未经雕饰的享受。人只有通过致力于社会，才能在生命短暂而危险的情况下找到生命的意义。

在我看来，当今资本主义社会所存在的经济无政府状态是真正的邪恶根源。我们看到在我们面前有一个庞大的生产者社区，其成员不断地试图以合法的规则忠实地遵守的方式相互剥夺他们集体劳动的成果。在这方面，重要的是要认识到生产手段——也就是说，生产消费品以及额外资本货物所需的全部生产能力——可能合法地并且在大多数情况下实际上是个人的私有财产。

为了简单起见，在接下来的讨论中，我将把所有那些不分享生产手段所有权的人称为“工人”——尽管这并不完全符合这个术语的惯用法。生产手段的所有者有能力购买工人的劳动力。通过使用生产手段，工人生产新的商品，这些商品成为资本家的财产。这一过程的关键点在于工人生产的东西和他所获得的报酬之间的关系，两者都以实际价值来衡量。就“自由”劳动合同而言，工人所获得的并不是由他生产的商品的实际价值决定的，而是由他的最低需求和资本家对劳动力的需求与竞争工作的工人数量之间的关系决定的。重要的是要理解，即使在理论上，工人的报酬也不是由他的产品的价值决定的。

私人资本倾向于集中在少数人手中，部分原因是资本家之间的竞争，部分原因是技术发展和劳动分工的增加促使生产单位的形成以牺牲较小的单位为代价。这些发展的结果是私人资本的寡头统治，其巨大的权力甚至无法通过民主组织的政治社会有效地制衡。这是真实的，因为立法机构的成员由政治党派选举产生，这些政党在很大程度上由私人资本家资助或以其他方式影响，实际上他们将选民与立法机构分开。结果是，人民的代表实际上并没有充分保护人口中弱势群体的利益。此外，在现有条件下，私人资本家不可避免地直接或间接控制着主要的信息来源（新闻、广播、教育）。因此，对于个别公民来说，客观地得出结论并明智地使用他的政治权利是极其困难的，而且在大多数情况下几乎是不可能的。

因此，基于私有资本的经济所普遍存在的情况由两个主要原则特征：首先，生产手段（资本）是私有的，所有者可以按照自己的意愿处置它们；其次，劳动合同是自由的。当然，没有所谓的纯粹的资本主义社会。特别是，应该注意到，工人通过长期而艰苦的政治斗争，已经成功地为某些工人类别争取到了“自由劳动合同”的某种改进形式。但总的来说，当今经济与“纯粹”的资本主义没有太大区别。

生产是为了利润而不是为了使用进行的。没有规定所有有能力并愿意工作的人总是能够找到工作的位置；几乎总是存在一个“失业军”。工人不断担心失去工作。由于失业和收入低下的工人不提供有利可图的市场，消费品的生产受到限制，因此造成了巨大的困难。技术进步常常导致更多的失业而不是为所有人减轻工作负担。利润动机，结合资本家之间的竞争，导致资本积累和利用的不稳定，从而导致越来越严重的经济萧条。无限制的竞争导致了巨大的劳动浪费，以及我之前提到的个人社会意识的削弱。

我认为这种对个人的削弱是资本主义最坏的邪恶。我们的整个教育系统都受到这种邪恶的影响。学生被灌输了一种夸张的竞争态度，他被训练为崇拜物质成功作为他未来职业的准备。

只有一种方法可以消除这个弊端，那就是计划经济，以及配套的教育系统。在这样的经济中，生产手段由社会本身拥有，并以计划的方式使用。计划经济，根据社区的需求调整生产，将要做的工作分配给所有能工作的人，并保证每个男人、女人和孩子的生计。个体的教育，除了促进他自己的天赋能力外，还会试图在他身上培养对同胞的责任感，而不是在我们当前社会中对权力和成功的颂扬。

然而，必须记住，计划经济还不是社会主义。计划经济本身可能伴随着个人的完全奴役。实现社会主义需要解决一些极其困难的社会政治问题：鉴于政治和经济权力的广泛集中，如何防止官僚主义变得全能和傲慢？如何保护个人的权利，并以此确保对官僚权力的民主制衡？

对社会主义的目标和问题有清晰的认识在我们这个转型时代至关重要。由于在当前情况下，这些问题的自由和不受阻碍的讨论已经受到强大的禁忌，我认为这本杂志的创立是一项重要的公共服务。

English Version

Is it advisable for one who is not an expert on economic and social issues to express views on the subject of socialism? I believe for a number of reasons that it is.

Let us first consider the question from the point of view of scientific knowledge. It might appear that there are no essential methodological differences between astronomy and economics: scientists in both fields attempt to discover laws of general acceptability for a circumscribed group of phenomena in order to make the interconnection of these phenomena as clearly understandable as possible. But in reality such methodological differences do exist. The discovery of general laws in the field of economics is made difficult by the circumstance that observed economic phenomena are often affected by many factors which are very hard to evaluate separately. In addition, the experience which has accumulated since the beginning of the so-called civilized period of human history has—as is well known—been largely influenced and limited by causes which are by no means exclusively economic in nature. For example, most of the major states of history owed their existence to conquest. The conquering peoples established themselves, legally and economically, as the privileged class of the conquered country. They seized for themselves a monopoly of the land ownership and appointed a priesthood from among their own ranks. The priests, in control of education, made the class division of society into a permanent institution and created a system of values by which the people were thenceforth, to a large extent unconsciously, guided in their social behavior.

But historic tradition is, so to speak, of yesterday; nowhere have we really overcome what Thorstein Veblen called “the predatory phase” of human development. The observable economic facts belong to that phase and even such laws as we can derive from them are not applicable to other phases. Since the real purpose of socialism is precisely to overcome and advance beyond the predatory phase of human development, economic science in its present state can throw little light on the socialist society of the future.

Second, socialism is directed towards a social-ethical end. Science, however, cannot create ends and, even less, instill them in human beings; science, at most, can supply the means by which to attain certain ends. But the ends themselves are conceived by personalities with lofty ethical ideals and—if these ends are not stillborn, but vital and vigorous—are adopted and carried forward by those many human beings who, half unconsciously, determine the slow evolution of society.

For these reasons, we should be on our guard not to overestimate science and scientific methods when it is a question of human problems; and we should not assume that experts are the only ones who have a right to express themselves on questions affecting the organization of society.

Innumerable voices have been asserting for some time now that human society is passing through a crisis, that its stability has been gravely shattered. It is characteristic of such a situation that individuals feel indifferent or even hostile toward the group, small or large, to which they belong. In order to illustrate my meaning, let me record here a personal experience. I recently discussed with an intelligent and well-disposed man the threat of another war, which in my opinion would seriously endanger the existence of mankind, and I remarked that only a supra-national organization would offer protection from that danger. Thereupon my visitor, very calmly and coolly, said to me: “Why are you so deeply opposed to the disappearance of the human race?”

I am sure that as little as a century ago no one would have so lightly made a statement of this kind. It is the statement of a man who has striven in vain to attain an equilibrium within himself and has more or less lost hope of succeeding. It is the expression of a painful solitude and isolation from which so many people are suffering in these days. What is the cause? Is there a way out?

It is easy to raise such questions, but difficult to answer them with any degree of assurance. I must try, however, as best I can, although I am very conscious of the fact that our feelings and strivings are often contradictory and obscure and that they cannot be expressed in easy and simple formulas.

Man is, at one and the same time, a solitary being and a social being. As a solitary being, he attempts to protect his own existence and that of those who are closest to him, to satisfy his personal desires, and to develop his innate abilities. As a social being, he seeks to gain the recognition and affection of his fellow human beings, to share in their pleasures, to comfort them in their sorrows, and to improve their conditions of life. Only the existence of these varied, frequently conflicting, strivings accounts for the special character of a man, and their specific combination determines the extent to which an individual can achieve an inner equilibrium and can contribute to the well-being of society. It is quite possible that the relative strength of these two drives is, in the main, fixed by inheritance. But the personality that finally emerges is largely formed by the environment in which a man happens to find himself during his development, by the structure of the society in which he grows up, by the tradition of that society, and by its appraisal of particular types of behavior. The abstract concept “society” means to the individual human being the sum total of his direct and indirect relations to his contemporaries and to all the people of earlier generations. The individual is able to think, feel, strive, and work by himself; but he depends so much upon society—in his physical, intellectual, and emotional existence—that it is impossible to think of him, or to understand him, outside the framework of society. It is “society” which provides man with food, clothing, a home, the tools of work, language, the forms of thought, and most of the content of thought; his life is made possible through the labor and the accomplishments of the many millions past and present who are all hidden behind the small word “society.”

It is evident, therefore, that the dependence of the individual upon society is a fact of nature which cannot be abolished—just as in the case of ants and bees. However, while the whole life process of ants and bees is fixed down to the smallest detail by rigid, hereditary instincts, the social pattern and interrelationships of human beings are very variable and susceptible to change. Memory, the capacity to make new combinations, the gift of oral communication have made possible developments among human being which are not dictated by biological necessities. Such developments manifest themselves in traditions, institutions, and organizations; in literature; in scientific and engineering accomplishments; in works of art. This explains how it happens that, in a certain sense, man can influence his life through his own conduct, and that in this process conscious thinking and wanting can play a part.

Man acquires at birth, through heredity, a biological constitution which we must consider fixed and unalterable, including the natural urges which are characteristic of the human species. In addition, during his lifetime, he acquires a cultural constitution which he adopts from society through communication and through many other types of influences. It is this cultural constitution which, with the passage of time, is subject to change and which determines to a very large extent the relationship between the individual and society. Modern anthropology has taught us, through comparative investigation of so-called primitive cultures, that the social behavior of human beings may differ greatly, depending upon prevailing cultural patterns and the types of organization which predominate in society. It is on this that those who are striving to improve the lot of man may ground their hopes: human beings are not condemned, because of their biological constitution, to annihilate each other or to be at the mercy of a cruel, self-inflicted fate.

If we ask ourselves how the structure of society and the cultural attitude of man should be changed in order to make human life as satisfying as possible, we should constantly be conscious of the fact that there are certain conditions which we are unable to modify. As mentioned before, the biological nature of man is, for all practical purposes, not subject to change. Furthermore, technological and demographic developments of the last few centuries have created conditions which are here to stay. In relatively densely settled populations with the goods which are indispensable to their continued existence, an extreme division of labor and a highly-centralized productive apparatus are absolutely necessary. The time—which, looking back, seems so idyllic—is gone forever when individuals or relatively small groups could be completely self-sufficient. It is only a slight exaggeration to say that mankind constitutes even now a planetary community of production and consumption.

I have now reached the point where I may indicate briefly what to me constitutes the essence of the crisis of our time. It concerns the relationship of the individual to society. The individual has become more conscious than ever of his dependence upon society. But he does not experience this dependence as a positive asset, as an organic tie, as a protective force, but rather as a threat to his natural rights, or even to his economic existence. Moreover, his position in society is such that the egotistical drives of his make-up are constantly being accentuated, while his social drives, which are by nature weaker, progressively deteriorate. All human beings, whatever their position in society, are suffering from this process of deterioration. Unknowingly prisoners of their own egotism, they feel insecure, lonely, and deprived of the naive, simple, and unsophisticated enjoyment of life. Man can find meaning in life, short and perilous as it is, only through devoting himself to society.

The economic anarchy of capitalist society as it exists today is, in my opinion, the real source of the evil. We see before us a huge community of producers the members of which are unceasingly striving to deprive each other of the fruits of their collective labor—not by force, but on the whole in faithful compliance with legally established rules. In this respect, it is important to realize that the means of production—that is to say, the entire productive capacity that is needed for producing consumer goods as well as additional capital goods—may legally be, and for the most part are, the private property of individuals.

For the sake of simplicity, in the discussion that follows I shall call “workers” all those who do not share in the ownership of the means of production—although this does not quite correspond to the customary use of the term. The owner of the means of production is in a position to purchase the labor power of the worker. By using the means of production, the worker produces new goods which become the property of the capitalist. The essential point about this process is the relation between what the worker produces and what he is paid, both measured in terms of real value. Insofar as the labor contract is “free,” what the worker receives is determined not by the real value of the goods he produces, but by his minimum needs and by the capitalists’ requirements for labor power in relation to the number of workers competing for jobs. It is important to understand that even in theory the payment of the worker is not determined by the value of his product.

Private capital tends to become concentrated in few hands, partly because of competition among the capitalists, and partly because technological development and the increasing division of labor encourage the formation of larger units of production at the expense of smaller ones. The result of these developments is an oligarchy of private capital the enormous power of which cannot be effectively checked even by a democratically organized political society. This is true since the members of legislative bodies are selected by political parties, largely financed or otherwise influenced by private capitalists who, for all practical purposes, separate the electorate from the legislature. The consequence is that the representatives of the people do not in fact sufficiently protect the interests of the underprivileged sections of the population. Moreover, under existing conditions, private capitalists inevitably control, directly or indirectly, the main sources of information (press, radio, education). It is thus extremely difficult, and indeed in most cases quite impossible, for the individual citizen to come to objective conclusions and to make intelligent use of his political rights.

The situation prevailing in an economy based on the private ownership of capital is thus characterized by two main principles: first, means of production (capital) are privately owned and the owners dispose of them as they see fit; second, the labor contract is free. Of course, there is no such thing as a pure capitalist society in this sense. In particular, it should be noted that the workers, through long and bitter political struggles, have succeeded in securing a somewhat improved form of the “free labor contract” for certain categories of workers. But taken as a whole, the present day economy does not differ much from “pure” capitalism.

Production is carried on for profit, not for use. There is no provision that all those able and willing to work will always be in a position to find employment; an “army of unemployed” almost always exists. The worker is constantly in fear of losing his job. Since unemployed and poorly paid workers do not provide a profitable market, the production of consumers’ goods is restricted, and great hardship is the consequence. Technological progress frequently results in more unemployment rather than in an easing of the burden of work for all. The profit motive, in conjunction with competition among capitalists, is responsible for an instability in the accumulation and utilization of capital which leads to increasingly severe depressions. Unlimited competition leads to a huge waste of labor, and to that crippling of the social consciousness of individuals which I mentioned before.

This crippling of individuals I consider the worst evil of capitalism. Our whole educational system suffers from this evil. An exaggerated competitive attitude is inculcated into the student, who is trained to worship acquisitive success as a preparation for his future career.

I am convinced there is only one way to eliminate these grave evils, namely through the establishment of a socialist economy, accompanied by an educational system which would be oriented toward social goals. In such an economy, the means of production are owned by society itself and are utilized in a planned fashion. A planned economy, which adjusts production to the needs of the community, would distribute the work to be done among all those able to work and would guarantee a livelihood to every man, woman, and child. The education of the individual, in addition to promoting his own innate abilities, would attempt to develop in him a sense of responsibility for his fellow men in place of the glorification of power and success in our present society.

Nevertheless, it is necessary to remember that a planned economy is not yet socialism. A planned economy as such may be accompanied by the complete enslavement of the individual. The achievement of socialism requires the solution of some extremely difficult socio-political problems: how is it possible, in view of the far-reaching centralization of political and economic power, to prevent bureaucracy from becoming all-powerful and overweening? How can the rights of the individual be protected and therewith a democratic counterweight to the power of bureaucracy be assured?

Clarity about the aims and problems of socialism is of greatest significance in our age of transition. Since, under present circumstances, free and unhindered discussion of these problems has come under a powerful taboo, I consider the foundation of this magazine to be an important public service.

质点、刚体、非刚体

今日，终于想明白了乒乓球中的旋转！

在玩乒乓球时，认为它是刚体就可以了。（质点，失之过简，那就没有旋转了；非刚体，过于复杂，只有当你研究生产新胶皮时，才需要用非刚体模型）

自由度

确定一个刚体的位置，需要6个自由度。

  • 第一个质点的$x, y, z$
  • 第二个质点的$x, y, z$，并加上一个约束
  • 第三个质点的$x, y, z$，并加上两个约束
  • 第四个质点的$x, y, z$，并加上三个约束
  • …
  • 第n个质点的x, y, z，并加上三个约束

确定一个刚体的运动，同样需要6个自由度。

  • 质心的$v_{x}, v_{y}, v_{z}$
  • 绕质心的旋转的旋转轴，需要2个自由度
  • 绕质心的旋转的角速度，需要1个自由度

在此，我们可以认为乒乓球的运动是2维的。确定其位置需要2个自由度，确定其运动需要2个自由度。（于是乎，我们简化掉了侧旋）

旋转有三种

下图称之为下旋 (back-spin)，下下图称之为上旋 (up-spin)，第三种是不旋转。

击球方式有三种

给球上旋、给球下旋、不给旋转。

上旋和下旋的转换

简化一下，不考虑球没有旋转的情况，以及不给球旋转的击球。

于是，球的旋转只有两种——上旋、下旋。

一共有四种情况

  • 当来球是上旋时，你可以选择给上旋或者给下旋转。

  • 当来球是下旋时，你同样可以选择给上旋或者给下旋转。

这四种情况的通俗叫法

  • 上旋+给球上旋：对拉弧圈
  • 上旋+给球下旋：削球
  • 下旋+给球上旋：拉弧圈
  • 下旋+给球下旋：搓球

饭店

2008年，中国，一座小城。

一对年轻的夫妻带着他们小学二年级的儿子走进一家西餐厅。在大城市，这家餐厅不算高档，但是在这座小城里，已经很奢侈了。小男孩在公交车上看到了它投放的广告，一直嚷着要吃牛排。

暖黄色的灯光洒在餐桌上，白色桌布，银色餐具，背景音乐轻轻流淌。顾客不多，散落在各处，安静地用餐。

不一会，热滋滋的牛排端了上来。服务员礼貌地询问是否需要帮忙切开，小男孩热切地表示自己会切。然而，他尝试了很久，却只能在牛排上留下浅浅的痕迹。最后他甚至用两只手一起往下压，但牛排依然无动于衷。爸爸笑了，把刀拿了过来，轻松地切下了一块。

“为什么你这么轻松就切下，而我却不行？”小男孩沮丧地问

“你是上下用力的，而我是前后用力的。“

“我和你爸爸第一次去吃西餐，他告诉我，这个现象可以用二维张量描述”，母亲笑着说。

“那是什么？”小男孩问

“大学学到的一个东西而已”母亲解释到

用餐结束后，保洁把三人的桌子擦得干干净净。

从餐厅回家的路上，小男孩一直不说话。

父亲和母亲都察觉到了异常。

父亲打破了沉默：“怎么了，饭吃得不开心吗？”

小男孩：“我在想，为什么服务生和保洁对我们这么客气？”

父亲：“嗯？”

小男孩：“我觉得仅仅是因为我们有钱。”

父亲和母亲有些诧异地看着他们的孩子。

小男孩继续说：“人生而平等，但是显然他们和我们是不平等的。因为，他们，还有他们的孩子，并不能去那个餐厅用餐。”

母亲说道：“他们现在吃不起，不代表他们以后也吃不起，也不代表他们的孩子以后也吃不起。他们凭借自己的努力挣到钱，不就可以了吗？”

小男孩：“但总要有人做这些工作。他们成为有钱人之后，依然会有其他人来做这些。”

母亲：“也许有一天，机器人可以代替这些工作。“

小男孩：”在那一天到来之前呢？似乎只要钱存在，就一定会有这些不平等的现象。”

父亲蹲了下来，摸了摸小男孩的头发，说：”你说的是有道理的，我小时候，也想过这些问题。妈妈也想过。”

小男孩急切地问：””那你们想出办法了吗？”

父亲问：“你对苏联有什么印象？”

小男孩说：“苏联是一个很强大的国家！它打败了邪恶的纳粹德国，拯救了世界，还是第一个把人类送进太空的国家！也是第一个把探测器送上月球的国家！”

小男孩的眼里闪过一抹亮色，兴奋地说：“前些天我看了一部纪录片，里面讲，科罗廖夫是苏联航天的英雄，他的骨灰被洒在了克里姆林宫前。”

父亲：“这些是很强大，但也许，苏联最强大的是，它拥有和现在世界上任何一个国家都不同的…嗯…经济制度。”

小男孩问：“是什么？”

父亲斟酌着词句：在苏联，大部分东西都是属于整个国家的，只有小部分是属于私人的。苏联也有钱和工资。但是，在饭店工作的服务员，每月的工资，足以让他享受饭店的服务。

父亲继续说：“这种情况称为公有制，而现在的称为私有制。我们中国，还有其他一些小国家，也都执行过类似的公有制制度。但是后来，他们都遇到了严重的问题，开始经济改革，转换成了现在的经济制度。”

“为什么？”男孩问

父亲：“说来话长，这件事，你也许需要好几年才能理解。”

男孩：“那，人类未来，到底会是私有制，还是公有制？”

父亲：“爷爷奶奶那一代没有给出答案，我们这一代也没有给出答案，也许你们这一代也不会，但是，一代代人之后，人类终究会做出自己的选择的。亦或者，像妈妈跟你说的那样，机器人技术迎来突破，人类和机器人一起组建新的社会，也说不定。”

母亲也蹲了下来，拉着男孩的手：“我们只要期待那一天的到来就好了。”

男孩也拉了拉妈妈的手，但依然有些不开心。

母亲继续说：“你作为餐厅的顾客，能关心其它人，已经很了不起了。刚刚爸爸和你说的那些，都太过宏大了。如果全人类是一台巨大的机器，我们都是这个机器上一颗小小的螺丝钉。终其一生，我们付出再多的努力，很可能也不会对这台机器造成任何的改变。一个人只要过好自己的生活，再量力而行地帮助身边的人，就是一个好人。”

男孩若有所思，但是依然不快。

母亲：“你爸爸上大学时候，也天天吹嘘自己要改变世界。但他现在只想着每天晚上吃什么。”

男孩终于乐了，用手敲着爸爸的头。

我的敏感词

Posted on 2024-01-18 In 2024 , 2024-01 Word count in article: 532 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.

应该、建议、查一下、你不够强

当别人对我说这四个词的时候，我会感到生气。

有时是心里小小地咯噔一下，有时是强烈的愤怒。

哪怕我明知道他/她并没有恶意，亦如此。

今天终于仔细思考了一下，了解了原因。

最简单的是”你不够强“。从小学开始，我一直很争强好胜。哪怕是玩CF、飞车、LOL，我也总希望自己比别人强，更不用说工作、学习、跑步、篮球。

其次是”应该“、”建议“。这两个词，第一是让我觉得他/她在质疑我的能力，第二是让我觉得我的自主性受到了挑战，第三是让我觉得他/她在用权威的语气对我说话。

最后是”查一下“。这个词，第一同样是让我觉得能力受到质疑，第二是让我回忆起了大一大二因为不擅长使用搜索引擎错失机会的往事。

我依然不喜欢别人说我不够强，依然不喜欢自主性受到挑战，依然不喜欢权威。

这是我的性格。

我一直觉得，性格若想改，是可以改的，并不是”江山易改本性难移“，我已经改了我性格中的很多缺点，比如情绪不稳定，比如刚愎自用，比如不喜欢分享真正的观点，比如不会换位思考，但这些都是我明确知道它们严重阻碍了我的进步，我才改的。以上两点，我不觉得它们有害，反而有利。

但是我不会单独因为他/她说了这四个词，而非常生气，而反驳他/她。前者对自己不好，后者对对方不好。

我会看他/她是无意间说了这四个词（80%-90%的情况），还是真诚地想为我好，还是有意地质疑我的能力、挑战我的自主性、装作权威，然后决定对他/她的态度。

其实我过去一年多也是这么做的，只是没有成文。现在成文。
